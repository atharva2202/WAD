Tennis Information

Tennis is a sport of skill and strategy, played on a rectangular court with a net in the center. Players use rackets to hit a rubber ball back and forth, aiming to score points by landing the ball within the opponent's court. Matches are won by winning sets, with each set consisting of games. Professional tennis players compete in prestigious tournaments like the Grand Slam events, while recreational players enjoy the game for its physical and mental benefits. Tennis requires agility, precision, and mental focus, making it a challenging yet rewarding sport.

Tennis Rules

  1. Players must win points to win games, games to win sets, and sets to win matches.
  2. The server must stand behind the baseline and hit the ball diagonally into the service box opposite them.
  3. If the ball is hit outside of the court boundaries or into the net, it is considered a fault.
  4. A player wins a point when the opponent cannot return the ball into the court or commits a fault.
  5. The match is typically best of three or five sets, depending on the level of play.
  6. Players change sides of the court after every odd-numbered game.
  7. A tiebreaker is played when a set reaches a certain score (usually 6-6) to determine the winner of the set.
  8. A player must win by two points in a tiebreaker.
  9. If a player touches the net or crosses over to the opponent's side, they lose the point.
  10. During a serve, the ball must land in the service box diagonally opposite the server.
  11. Players are allowed to challenge calls made by the umpire using video replay technology.
  12. A player can request a medical timeout for a maximum of 3 minutes if injured during a match.
  13. If a player fails to return to the court within the allowed time after a break, they may face penalties.
  14. In doubles matches, partners must alternate hitting the ball, and the team must decide who returns the serve.
  15. Players are allowed a maximum of 25 seconds between points for serving.
  16. If a player disrupts their opponent's play intentionally, they may receive a warning or penalty.
